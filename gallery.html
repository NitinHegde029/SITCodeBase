Our Present ECO Club Activities :


Our ECO Club presently working towards development of Dr. Sree Sree Sivakumara Swamiji Bio Diversity Park at the land bearing Survey No. 20/2, measuring with an extent of 8.16 Acres, situated at Pandithanahalli Village, Urdigere Hobli, Tumakuru. This Bio Diversity Park is situated beside Mandaragiri(Basadi Betta) and Behind Mydala Lake. Siddaganga Institute of Technology is the first institution in the country which undertakes the development of Bio-Diversity Park that too in its own land.
This Bio Diversity Park was inaugurated by His Holiness Sree Siddalinga Maha Swamiji, President of Sree Siddaganga Education Society, Siddaganga Math, Kyathasandra, Tumakuru on 12-08-2020.
Dr. C N Ashwath Narayan, Deputy Chief Minister and Hon’ble Minister for Higher Education, Government of Karnataka, Sri Madhu Swamy J C, Hon’ble Minister for Law and Parliamentary Affairs, Government of Karnataka and Sri G.B. Jyothi Ganesh, Member of Legislative Assembly, Tumakuru constituency, Tumakuru were presided as our chief guests during inauguration of Dr. Sree Sree Sivakumara Swamiji Bio Diversity Park.
Special invitees Dr. Rakesh Kumar, Deputy Commissioner, Tumakuru, Sri Ajay V, Assistant Commissioner, Tumakuru, Dr. K. Vamsi Krishna, Superintendent of Police, Tumakuru, Dr. T.S. Aghora, Member Secretary, IRC, Indian Institute of Horticultural Research, Hesaraghatta, Bengaluru, Sri H.C Girish, Deputy Conservator of Forest, Department of Forest, Tumakuru, Sri T.K Nanjundappa, Secretary, Sree Siddaganga Education Society, Siddaganga Math, Tumakuru, Dr. M.N.Channabasappa, Director, Siddaganga Institute of Technology, Tumakuru and Dr. Shivakumariah, CEO, Siddaganga Institute of Technology, Tumakuru were also presided during the inauguration of Dr. Sree Sree Sivakumara Swamiji Bio Diversity park.
Dr. K P.Shivananda, Principal, S.I.T., Tumakuru delivered the welcome speech and Dr. S.V.Dinesh, Coordinator, Center for Rural Development, S.I.T., Tumakuru performed master of ceremony.
Dr. Rudramurthy M S, Associate Professor, Department of Information Science & Engineering and Coordinator, ECO Club, SIT Campus, Tumakuru briefed about the development of Dr. Sree Sree Sivakumara Swamiji Biodiversity Park and its benefits to stakeholders.
We have ample opportunity to develop this Bio diversity park and its surrounding area as a prominent Bio Sphere which protects our Mydala Lake. Within the vicinity of this Bio Diversity park we have Ramadevara Betta(Extent of land measuring 50 Acres) at mydala panchayath, the Gomala land bearing Sy. No 17 (Extent of land measuring 200 Acres) at pemmanahalli village, the Gomala land bearing Sy. No.17 (Extent of land measuring 98 Acres) at Pandithanalli Village, Siddaganga Betta and Devarayana Durga reserve forest. Therefore, the development of this BioDiversity park promotes development of 200 Acres of Bio-Sphere. During COVID-19 pandemic situation in the country i.e., during lockdown period, Dr. Rudramurthy M S, Coordinator and Principal Investigator did not remained silent. He has visited the various forest nurseries and engaged communication with the Department of Forest, obtained the permission from the Principal Conservator of Forest, Government of Karnataka and support from the Sri Sandeep Dave, Principal Secretary, Government of Karnataka. He has collected 3000 Tree Seedlings from various nurseries which belongs to the Department of Forest, Government of Karnataka under Hasiru Karnataka Scheme during the month of July 2020. After the inauguration of Dr. Sree Sree Sivakumara Swamiji Bio-Diversity Park, 2000 tree seedlings are planted by the faculty members of the ECO Club, SIT Campus, Tumakuru during the month of August 2020. Many other developmental activities are pending towards the development of full pledged Bio-diversity Park.

Our Past ECO Club Activities :


During the month of June 2020, Dr.Rudramurthy M S, Coordinator of the ECO Club visited Mandagadde Bird Sanctuary situated in an Island on Tunga river bund. Mandagadde Bird Sanctuary is one among 20 important Bird Sanctuary in the country. Around 5000 bird nestings can be seen during its peak.
